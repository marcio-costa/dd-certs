# Adaptive Weak-Area Drill — Protocol

The adaptive drill is a **personalized, focused** simulation that grinds the topics where you are weakest. Unlike domain drills (fixed scope) and the mock exam (full scope), this one shrinks to where you struggle.

## When to run it
1. After **every Full Mock Exam** (Days 21 and 27) — use the per-domain breakdown to identify weak areas.
2. Any time **two or more daily mini-quizzes in a row** drop below 7/10 on the same domain.
3. The **24h before** the real exam, as a final tightening pass.

## Protocol — 4 steps

### Step 1 — Build your weak-areas list
Open `weak-areas.md` (your running log). For each domain, count how many questions you've missed in the past two weeks. The two domains with the highest miss-count are your weak areas.

### Step 2 — Pick the drill size
| Total miss count | Drill size |
|---|---|
| Under 5 | 10 questions |
| 5 – 15 | 15 questions |
| Over 15 | 20–25 questions |

### Step 3 — Build the drill from the bank
Open `adaptive-bank.md` (the next file). Each question is tagged with its domain (`[D1]`, `[D2]`, …) and a sub-topic. Pick:
- ~70% of questions from your weakest domain
- ~20% from your second weakest domain
- ~10% review questions from any other domain

### Step 4 — Execute
- Time yourself (~1 minute / question)
- Score yourself afterward
- For every wrong answer, **re-read the relevant knowledge-base section the same day** and write a one-paragraph explanation in your own words
