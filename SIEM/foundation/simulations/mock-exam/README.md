# Full Mock Exams (75 Q / 120 min each)

Two full-length mock exams that mirror the real exam format and approximate domain weight.

| File | When to take | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `mock-exam-1.md` | Day 21 | Mid-program checkpoint. Knowledge-recall focused. |
| `mock-exam-2.md` | Day 27 | Final-week dress rehearsal. More scenario-based. |

## Real exam vs these mocks

| Feature | Real exam | These mocks |
|---|---|---|
| Total questions | 60 (50 scored + 10 pretest) | 75 |
| Time | 120 min | 120 min |
| Format | Multiple choice, single answer | Multiple choice, single answer |
| Domains | 7 | 7 |
| Pass mark | Not officially published; ~70% target | Same target |

The mocks are 75 Q (15 more than the real exam) on purpose — extra practice volume, and aligned with the larger 75-scored Datadog Fundamentals format used by other Datadog exams in the family.

## Conditions to follow when taking a mock
1. **Single sitting** — no breaks longer than 5 minutes.
2. **No notes** — close the knowledge base, no Datadog docs.
3. **Timer on** — 120 minutes max. After 90 minutes, lock in your top picks for any remaining questions.
4. **Answer everything** — unanswered = wrong on the real exam.
5. **Score honestly** — wrong answers are gold for tuning your prep.

## After the mock

1. Compute your **per-domain score** using the breakdown table at the bottom of each file.
2. Identify your **weakest 2 domains**.
3. Run an **Adaptive Weak-Area Drill** on those domains the next day.
4. Read the relevant **knowledge-base** sections again.
5. Move on to the next study day or sit the second mock.

If your Mock #2 result is **≥ 70%** with no domain below 60%, you are ready to sit the real exam.
